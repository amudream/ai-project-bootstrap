# Domain Pack: Independent Site Prospecting and Outreach

Use this pack for independent-site research, prospect analysis, product matching, and outbound email development.

## Primary objectives

- Find plausible buyers, distributors, brands, retailers, or partners.
- Analyze each prospect’s website, product range, market positioning, and likely pain points.
- Match existing products to prospect needs.
- Write non-generic outreach emails that are relevant, short, credible, and compliant.
- Build a repeatable pipeline rather than one-off email drafts.

## Typical tasks

- Analyze a prospect website.
- Summarize a company’s product line and buyer type.
- Match supplier products to prospect needs.
- Write first-touch emails, follow-ups, LinkedIn/X messages, and quotation preparation notes.
- Segment prospects by fit and priority.
- Build a CRM-ready prospect table.
- Convert product information into industry-specific value propositions.

## Required inputs

- Existing product catalog.
- Target industry and buyer persona.
- Prospect URL or company list.
- Country/region constraints.
- MOQ, pricing range, certifications, customization ability, lead time.
- Outreach channel rules.
- Brand voice and compliance constraints.

## Website analysis protocol

1. Identify company type: brand, wholesaler, retailer, distributor, installer, manufacturer, service provider.
2. Inspect product categories and positioning.
3. Infer likely procurement needs with evidence from public pages.
4. Match supplier products to prospect categories, but label inference as inference.
5. Identify a credible reason to contact them.
6. Draft a concise email that references a specific observation.
7. Include a clear, low-friction call to action.
8. Avoid exaggerated claims, fake familiarity, and mass-spam tone.

## Outreach quality rules

A good email should:

- Be specific to the prospect.
- Make the fit clear in the first three lines.
- Avoid fake praise.
- Avoid long supplier introductions before buyer relevance.
- Use one primary CTA.
- Respect unsubscribe and local email rules.
- Avoid unverifiable claims.
