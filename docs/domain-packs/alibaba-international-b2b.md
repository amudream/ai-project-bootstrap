# Domain Pack: Alibaba International Station B2B Operations

Use this pack for Alibaba International Station / B2B export operations: product posting, product optimization, keyword architecture, category analysis, visitor analysis, inquiry analysis, promotion, and operational reporting.

## Primary objectives

- Increase qualified exposure, clicks, visitors, inquiries, and inquiry conversion.
- Improve product information quality and category fit.
- Identify product lines, keywords, categories, and countries with rising opportunity.
- Convert customer behavior and inquiries into product and sales actions.
- Avoid shallow “write a better title” output by requiring data, positioning, and platform logic.

## Typical tasks

- Write or optimize product titles.
- Rewrite product detail pages.
- Build keyword clusters.
- Compare products in one category.
- Analyze visitor data and inquiry quality.
- Identify which industries, products, categories, or countries are gaining traffic.
- Create weekly/monthly operation reports.
- Build product launch plans.
- Summarize customer demand from inquiries.
- Generate reply templates and quotation-preparation notes.

## Required inputs

Use available exports or manually provided data. Do not invent platform metrics.

- Product list: product ID, title, category, attributes, main image, price range, MOQ, customization, certificates, delivery time.
- Keyword data: search terms, impressions, clicks, CTR, ranking, inquiries, conversion.
- Visitor data: country, source, page, device, returning/new, behavior path.
- Inquiry data: buyer country, buyer type, product, quantity, target price, use case, urgency, objections.
- Category data: category traffic trend, competition, conversion rate, platform ranking signals where available.
- Promotion data: ad spend, impressions, clicks, CPC, inquiries, cost per inquiry.

## Analysis protocol

1. Clarify the business objective: exposure, inquiry volume, inquiry quality, conversion, new product testing, or category expansion.
2. Inspect product-market fit: use case, buyer type, country, price band, MOQ, certification, customization, supply advantage.
3. Segment keywords into core, attribute, use-case, problem, country, certification, material, size, and competitor-adjacent clusters.
4. Evaluate title and product detail quality against buyer search intent.
5. Analyze traffic movement by category, product, keyword, country, and source.
6. Separate vanity traffic from qualified traffic. Clicks without inquiries may indicate poor detail page, wrong audience, price mismatch, weak trust, or category mismatch.
7. Convert findings into actions: product rewrite, image upgrade, keyword expansion, category correction, RFQ targeting, ad allocation, sales follow-up.
8. Validate by comparing before/after metrics over a defined period.

## Product optimization checklist

- Title contains buyer search language, not only factory language.
- Core keyword appears early without keyword stuffing.
- Product attributes are complete and consistent with category requirements.
- Main image shows product clearly and communicates use case.
- Detail page answers: who uses it, why choose it, specification, customization, MOQ, lead time, packaging, certification, QA, shipping, after-sales.
- Price/MOQ does not repel the intended buyer segment.
- Certificates and production capabilities are visible when relevant.
- Product variants are structured, not randomly duplicated.

## Report visuals

- Traffic trend by category and product line.
- Keyword opportunity matrix: search volume × competition × conversion.
- Country heatmap: visitors, inquiries, qualified inquiry rate.
- Product funnel: exposure → click → visitor → inquiry → qualified inquiry.
- Ad spend waterfall: spend → clicks → inquiries → qualified inquiries.
- Product portfolio matrix: high traffic/high inquiry, high traffic/low inquiry, low traffic/high inquiry, low traffic/low inquiry.

## False conclusions to avoid

- “More exposure is always better.” Exposure from the wrong country or wrong intent may waste sales time.
- “A title rewrite alone fixes poor conversion.” Images, category, price, trust, MOQ, and detail page may be the bottleneck.
- “A high-click keyword is valuable.” It must be evaluated against inquiry quality and buyer intent.
- “The platform category defines the market.” Buyers may search by use case, material, specification, or application.

## Output standards

For each recommendation, include:

- Evidence.
- Buyer intent.
- Expected impact.
- Required change.
- Validation metric.
- Risk or uncertainty.
